<p><p>Automotive Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) systems come in two main types: Belt-Chain Drive CVT and Planetary CVT. Belt-Chain Drive CVT uses a flexible belt or chain to smoothly vary gear ratios, offering a more efficient and smooth driving experience. On the other hand, Planetary CVT employs a system of gears and pulleys to achieve the same result. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in modern vehicles to provide better fuel efficiency and performance.</p></p>
