Polyethylene-based fabrics are lightweight and flexible, offering high resistance to impact and penetration. Aramid-based fabrics, such as Kevlar, are known for their extreme strength and durability, making them ideal for ballistic protection. Both types of fabrics are commonly used in the production of bulletproof vests, helmets, vehicle armor, and other protective gear for military, law enforcement, and civilian applications.</p></p>
