<p><p>Large-scale LNG terminals are categorized based on their primary function: liquefaction and regasification. Liquefaction terminals are used to convert natural gas into liquid form for transportation and storage, while regasification terminals convert LNG back into its gaseous state for distribution and use. Both types of terminals can be located either onshore, built on land near coastal areas, or offshore, constructed on floating structures in the ocean. Each type of terminal plays a crucial role in the LNG supply chain, facilitating the efficient transportation and utilization of natural gas resources.</p></p>
