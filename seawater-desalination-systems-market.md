<p><p>Seawater desalination systems are used to remove salt and other impurities from seawater to make it suitable for drinking and other uses. The three main types of desalination systems are Reverse Osmosis (RO), Multistage Flash (MSF), and Multi-Effect Distillation (MED). RO uses a membrane to filter out salt, MSF involves heating seawater to create steam, and MED uses multiple stages of evaporation to purify water. Each method has its own advantages and drawbacks in terms of efficiency, cost, and environmental impact.</p></p>
