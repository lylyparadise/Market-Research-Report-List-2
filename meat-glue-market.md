<p><p>Meat glue, also known as transglutaminase, is a unique enzyme that can be sourced from animals, plants, or microbial organisms. Animal-derived meat glue is typically obtained from blood plasma, while plant-based options are derived from fermented soybeans. Microbial meat glue comes from bacteria or fungi through fermentation processes. Each type of meat glue offers different benefits and applications in the food industry, such as improving texture, binding ingredients together, and enhancing the appearance of meat products.</p></p>
