<p><p>Lightweight architecture refers to the design and construction of structures that prioritize weight reduction, minimal material usage, and energy efficiency. Lightweight architecture is increasingly being adopted in various sectors including residential, commercial, and industrial buildings.</p><p>The global lightweight architecture market is experiencing significant growth, with a projected CAGR of 7.2% during the forecast period. These materials are being increasingly used in the construction of lightweight structures that require greater structural integrity and energy efficiency. Moreover, the integration of digital technologies such as Building Information Modeling (BIM) and computational design tools is enabling architects and engineers to optimize the design of lightweight buildings for improved performance and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
